## Rationale

This assessment, undertaken both individually and collaboratively in a small group, offers a unique opportunity for you to grasp fundamental concepts crucial for your evolution into a proficient computing professional. By engaging in this task, you delve into the essentials of UX development and Unity, skills integral for future workplace scenarios. The assessment mirrors real-world scenarios where you will be required to investigate requirements, collaborate with a team in application design, and collectively develop a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) incorporating interface elements, media resources, and interactive features. While Assessment 1 is an individual effort, it lays the groundwork for effective communication with team members, anticipating the collaborative dynamics essential for Assessment 2.


## Task Description

This assessment involves two main tasks: collaborating with your team to design a shared menu scene for the final project and creating an individual Unity greybox prototype scene.

The simple grey box 3D project will include:

• Simple functionality;

• Minimal feedback;

• Assets you create within Unity;

• A menu;

• A scene that will resemble the idea for your final Assessment 2.


PROG2001 Assessment 1


The greybox prototype will be built as a WebGL project and uploaded to itch.io. You will also need to provide the itch.io link to Blackboard to receive feedback from other students about the project.



## Task Instructions

### Steps

• Contact your team members and discuss the overall design, menu, navigation and theme. (While you are collaborating with your team, you will develop these ideas individually).

• Create your own project and design documentation including storyboards and mood board.

• Develop the menu, navigation and prototype scene in Unity.

Do not focus on getting any major code implemented at this stage. In addition to UX best practices, functionality, feedback, navigation, and team collaboration will also be marked. There are two parts of the marking criteria:

• Technical competency in developing a minimum viable product;

• The level of professional UX development shown.

The difference between a "Pass" and higher grades will be based on how much you look at the whole project and development practices, and how well you follow best practices while keeping your Unity project cohesive and effective.

### Description of SCU Grades

### High Distinction:

The student's performance, in addition to satisfying all of the basic learning requirements, demonstrates distinctive insight and ability in researching, analysing and applying relevant skills and concepts, and shows exceptional ability to synthesise, integrate and evaluate knowledge. The student's performance could be described as outstanding in relation to the learning requirements specified.

### Distinction:

The student's performance, in addition to satisfying all of the basic learning requirements, demonstrates distinctive insight and ability in researching, analysing and applying relevant skills and concepts, and shows a well-developed ability to synthesise, integrate and evaluate knowledge. The student's performance could be described as distinguished in relation to the learning requirements specified.

### Credit:

The student's performance, in addition to satisfying all of the basic learning requirements specified, demonstrates insight and ability in researching, analysing and applying relevant skills and concepts. The student's performance could be described as competent in relation to the learning requirements specified.

### Pass:

The student's performance satisfies all of the basic learning requirements specified and provides a sound basis for proceeding to higher-level studies in the subject area. The student's performance could be described as satisfactory in relation to the learning requirements specified.

### Fail:

The student's performance fails to satisfy the learning requirements specified.
